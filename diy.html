CREATING DIY ACCESSORIES AND TOYS FOR YOUR DOG

On your computer, give these colorful card a quick hover!

1. BRAIDED T-SHIRT ROPE TOY

MATERIALS

Old cotton T-shirts (2-3) Scissors

STEPS

Cut the T-shirts into 3 long strips (about 2 inches wide) Tie the strips together at one end and secure to a sturdy surface. Braid the strips tightly, then tie another knot at the end.

BEST FOR

Dogs who love tug-of-war or chewing.

2. NO-SEW FLEECE DOG BANDANA

MATERIALS

Fleece fabric (a square, size depending on your dog) Scissors

STEPS

Fold the square diagonally to form a triangle. Cut a small slit near the folded edge (for the collar). Slide your dog’s collar through the slit to secure the bandana.

BEST FOR

A quick, stylish accessory—no sewing needed!

3. TENNIS BALL TREAT DISPENSER

MATERIALS

Tennis ball (or any sturdy rubber ball) Utility knife or scissors Small treats

STEPS

Carefully cut a small slit in the tennis ball (big enough for treats to fall out). Insert treats inside and let your dog roll it around to release the snacks.

BEST FOR

Mental stimulation and slow feeding.

4. DIY ROPE LEASH OR COLLAR

MATERIALS

Thick cotton rope (paracord works well) Clip or buckle (from an old leash) Scissors & lighter (to seal ends)

STEPS

Measure your dog’s neck (for a collar) or desired leash length. Braid or weave the rope, attaching the clip/buckle. Melt the ends with a lighter to prevent fraying.

BEST FOR

A durable, customizable leash or collar.

5. SOCK PLUSH TOY (WITH CRINKLE)

MATERIALS

Old sock Plastic bag or crinkle paper Stuffing (old fabric or polyfill)

STEPS

Crumple a plastic bag or crinkle paper and place it inside the sock. Add stuffing to fill the sock, then tie a knot at the open end. For extra fun, tie a knot in the middle to create a ball shape.

BEST FOR

Dogs who love squeaky or crinkly sounds.
