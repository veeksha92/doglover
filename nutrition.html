FOOD VIBES: WHAT SHOULD MY DOG EAT?

1. THE MAIN DISH: PICKING THE RIGHT FOOD!

PRESCRIPTION DIETS

For dogs with medical conditions (kidney disease, allergies, etc.).

KIBBLE (DRY FOOD)

Convenient, helps with dental health (some brands have tartar control).

WET FOOD

Higher moisture content, good for hydration but can be pricier.

RAW DIET

Controversial; may improve coat health but risks bacterial infections if not handled properly.

HOMEMADE MEALS

Requires vet-approved recipes to ensure balanced nutrition.

2. MUST-HAVE NUTRIENTS!

FIBER

Supports digestion and gut health (pumpkin, carrots, or vet-approved supplements).

PROTEIN

High-quality animal protein (chicken, beef, fish) should be the main ingredient.

HEALTHY FATS

Omega-3 and Omega-6 for skin, coat, and brain health (fish oil, flaxseed).

CARBS

Whole grains (brown rice, oats) or veggies (sweet potatoes, peas) for energy.

VITAMINS & MINERALS

Calcium, phosphorus, and vitamins A, D, E, and K are essential.

3. NO-NO FOODS!

SWEET POSIONS

Never feed your dog chocolate, grapes, raisins, or anything containing xylitol(sugar-free gum)—these can cause poisoning or even death.

TOXIC ALLIUMS

Onions, garlic, and related foods are toxic to dogs and can damage their red blood cells, leading to anemia.

HUMAN PARTY FAVORS

Keep your dog away from alcohol, caffeine, and avocados, which can cause vomiting, seizures, or heart failure.

CHOKING HAZARDS

Avoid giving dogs cooked bones, macadamia nuts, or fatty human foods,causes choking, pancreatitis, or neurological issues.

SALTY SNACK SABOTAGE

Avoid giving your dog salty snacks as too much sodium can lead to dehydration, kidney damage, or even sodium ion poisoning.

VET CHECK: KEEPING YOUR PUP IN TOP SHAPE

1. REGULAR VET VISIT: LESS STRESS LATER!

YEARLY CHECKUPS

Annual vet visits for vaccinations, dental checks, and weight monitoring.

SENIOR DOGS(+7)

Biannual check-ups for early disease detection.

PUPPY WELLNESS VISITS

New pups need monthly check-ups for vaccines, deworming, and growth monitoring until about 16 weeks old.

DENTAL CLEANING

Professional cleanings every 1-2 years prevent painful gum disease and tooth loss (bad breath isn't normal!).

PARASITE PREVENTION

Year-round flea/tick/heartworm meds with fecal tests at check-ups (yes, even for indoor dogs!).

2. EMERGENCY ALERT!

VOMITING/DIARRHEA

Rush your dog to the vet if they experience non-stop vomiting or diarrhea, as this could lead to dangerous dehydration or indicate poisoning.

DRINKING/PEEING TOO MUCH

Excessive thirst and urination could indicate kidney failure, diabetes, or toxins—don't wait to get this checked.

SEIZURES

Seizures, choking, or difficulty breathing are life-threatening emergencies that require immediate professional care.

SWOLLEN BELLY

A suddenly swollen or hard belly (especially in large breeds) may signal deadly bloat (GDV), which needs emergency surgery within hours.

TRAUMA ALERT

After severe trauma (car accidents, falls, or obvious broken bones), seek emergency care since internal injuries may not be visible.
