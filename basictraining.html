BASIC TRAINING

Shake Hands

Hold a treat in your closed hand near your dog's paw. When they paw at your hand, say "shake" and reward them. Repeat until they offer their paw on command.

Roll Over

Start with your dog in a 'down' position. Hold a treat near their nose and slowly move it towards their shoulder, then over their back. Reward when they complete the roll.

High Five

Once your dog knows 'shake', raise your hand slightly higher and say "high five". Reward when they touch your hand with their paw. Gradually increase the height.

Spin

Hold a treat near your dog's nose and slowly move it in a circle. Say "spin" as they follow the treat. Reward when they complete the circle. Teach both directions!

Play Dead

From a 'down' position, move a treat from their nose to the side until they roll onto their side. Add a hand signal (like a finger gun) and say "bang!" for dramatic effect.

Leg Weave

Stand with legs apart and lure your dog through with a treat. Start with one step, then gradually increase. Say "weave" as they move between your legs.

BASIC TRAINING FOR FIXING BEHAVIORS

Potty Training

Set up a regular routine for taking toilet breaks.Regularly take your dog outside, particularly after naps, playing, or meals.Use positive reinforcement when your dog eliminates outside.

Crate Training

Make the crate a place where your dog feels safe.To get your dog to go into the crate voluntarily, give them praise and goodies.To make your dog feel more at peace, gradually extend the amount of time they spend in the crate.

Teaching Commands(Sit and Stay)

A treat can be used to entice your dog to obey the "sit" instruction.Give your dog a reward as soon as they sit. Have your dog sit for "stay," then progressively extend the time and distance before rewarding them.

Leash Manners

To keep your dog under control when walking, use a short leash.Give your dog a treat if they follow you without dragging.To educate your dog to pay attention to you while wearing a leash, practice pausing and beginning.

Socialization

Introduce your dog to different situations, people, and other canines.Reward composure in social situations by using positive reinforcement. To assist your dog feel more at ease, gradually increase the amount of exposure.

Redirecting Undesirable Behaviors

Determine what causes undesirable habits.To refocus your dog's attention, offer alternate behaviors or diversions, such as toys or orders.Reward your dog when he or she chooses the right behavior over the bad one.
